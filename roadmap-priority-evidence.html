Why Miro Should Prioritise Building a Roadmap Solution

Data-Backed Evidence Brief

Roadmapping Investment Case February 2026 Internal Only
TL;DR
Data from the active sales pipeline, customer feedback, and website signals all converge on the same conclusion: there is validated, quantified demand for a Miro roadmapping solution. At least 21 customers ($3.3M existing ARR) have explicitly requested it, >$1.6M in immediate pipeline is attached to it, 60% of Miro users already use the product for roadmapping (with the highest frustration score of any use case), and the roadmapping template/landing pages are consistently among the highest-traffic content on miro.com.

Additional patterns from Gong call analysis:

  • Many want roadmaps that roll up epics/initiatives and show dependencies across teams (currently done in Monday/Asana/Jira Advanced Roadmaps)

2.5 Competitive Displacement Opportunity

Multiple signals indicate competitors are vulnerable and customers are actively seeking alternatives:

  • Productboard is deprecating legacy ARR boards — customers are actively evaluating alternatives including Miro Insights
  • Aha! is described as "roadmap-centric but rigid and siloed, not decision-oriented" — Miro can differentiate
  • The product roadmap software market is projected to reach $2.5 billion by 2031
  • The broader addressable market for roadmapping and portfolio management is $1B+
  • No competitor combines visual collaboration, two-way Jira integration, AI-powered customer insights, and infinite canvas flexibility
Source: Strat Pack H1FY27 Vision → Plan (Rosanna Knottenbelt)
Source: Weekly Tracker: Strategy and Execution Products (Vitaly Selkin)

4.2 Strategic Fit — The "Pillar 2" of Strategy & Execution

Roadmapping is explicitly named as Pillar 2 of the Strategy & Execution product vision:

  • Pillar 2: Roadmap Management — "Flexible roadmap views (timeline, swimlane, outcome-based)"
  • The full chain is: Strategy → Goals → Roadmaps → Projects → Tasks
  • "Tasks → Roadmaps: You cannot have an accurate roadmap without reliably structured tasks that track progress and timelines"
  • "No team ships in isolation; every roadmap item must map to the same end-to-end project journey"
