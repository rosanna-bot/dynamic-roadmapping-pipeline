Overview

Design Philosophy

Traditional roadmapping tools are reactive. They display what the PM enters and update only when the PM makes changes. Our system inverts this model.

1

Continuously Ingests

Signals from five distinct source categories

2

Analyzes & Correlates

Cross-source patterns invisible to manual review

3

Proactively Surfaces

Actionable insights at the right time, in the right context

4

Recommends Actions

Evidence-grounded, with humans firmly in control

Foundations

Architecture Principles

Proactive, not reactive

Surfaces insights without being asked — continuous intelligence, not query-response

Evidence-grounded

Every suggestion traceable to specific data points — customer quotes, velocity metrics, competitor actions

Human-in-the-loop

AI recommends; humans decide. Never makes autonomous roadmap changes without approval

Composable intelligence

Individual agent behaviors combine for complex analyses (e.g., Opportunity + Scenario = "What if?")

Progressive trust

Start with alerts and recommendations; evolve toward delegated actions as confidence builds

Privacy-aware

Respect data access boundaries; leaders see aggregated signals, not raw data they shouldn't access

Signal Source 1

Customer Voice

The richest and most unique signal source, powered by Miro Insights.

Data Sources

Gong transcripts, Salesforce notes, Zendesk tickets, NPS, app reviews, Qualtrics, in-app feedback, interviews

Signals Produced

Emerging themes, volume trends, sentiment shifts, request frequency, pain point clustering, feature demand ranking

Cadence

Real-time ingestion → daily clustering → weekly trend analysis

Raw data: 47 Zendesk tickets "export" + 12 Gong calls "reporting" ↓ AI clustering: "Reporting & Export" — volume up 60% MoM ↓ Enrichment: 23 accounts, $4.2M ARR, sentiment: frustrated ↓ Insight: "Reporting & Export is a top-5 pain this month. 23 accounts ($4.2M ARR). No roadmap coverage."
Signal Source 2

Execution Reality

Real-time data from engineering execution that reveals what's actually happening.

Data Sources

Jira/Azure DevOps (sprint data, epic progress, blockers), GitHub/GitLab (commits, PRs, code review)

Signals Produced

Sprint velocity trends, epic completion forecasts with confidence intervals, blocker patterns, scope change rate, dependency status

AI Processing

Time-series analysis, predictive delivery modeling, blocker pattern recognition, anomaly detection

Raw data: Team Alpha velocity: 45, 42, 38, 31, 28 ↓ AI analysis: 38% velocity decline over 5 sprints ↓ Correlation: 3 engineers reassigned + 2 blockers × 3 sprints ↓ Prediction: "Enterprise SSO" misses Q2 by 4 weeks ↓ Insight: "Recommend: resolve blockers (2-wk recovery) or adjust scope (cut Phase 2)."
Signal Sources 3 – 5

Market, Business & Strategy Signals

Market Context

Competitor tracking, analyst reports, news feeds, regulatory updates.

Example: Competitor X launches AI assistant → maps to 3 roadmap items → 12 accounts mentioned it in Gong calls → Consider accelerating AI Copilot from Q3.

Business Metrics

Product analytics, revenue dashboards, churn tracking, activation & retention.

Example: Timeline Widget adoption plateaus at 19% (target 30%) → correlated pain: "grouping" (200+ mentions) → 3 churned accounts → Accelerate improvements.

Strategy Artifacts

Company OKRs, department goals, initiative definitions, strategy decks.

Example: "Improve Enterprise Onboarding NPS" → only 15% capacity coverage → that initiative is 3 sprints behind → 15 accounts ($4.2M) flagged pain → OKR at risk.

Agent 1

Alignment Monitor

Mission: Ensure every piece of active work serves an active strategic goal, and every goal has sufficient investment.

Trigger Conditions

  • New work item added without goal linkage
  • Goal-initiative alignment score drops below threshold
  • Goal created without roadmap coverage
  • Scope changes reduce goal alignment
  • Goal retired but associated work continues

Key Outputs

  • Alignment score per initiative (real-time)
  • Orphan alerts (immediate)
  • Goal coverage gaps (weekly digest)
  • Re-alignment recommendations
Orphan detected: "Initiative 'Dark Mode Support' (Team Beta, 20% capacity) has no connection to any active Q2 goal. Linked goal was retired last month."
Coverage gap: "Goal 3 ('Enterprise Growth') has only 10% roadmap coverage — down from 35% after re-prioritization. 15 enterprise accounts are tracking features tied to this goal."
Drift alert: "Initiative 'Platform Redesign' scope expanded 40%. Alignment dropped from 85% to 52%. New items added without goal linkage."
Agent 2

Risk Predictor

Mission: Identify delivery risks before they materialize, giving PMs and leaders time to mitigate.

Risk Scoring Model

Low

On track; velocity stable; no blockers. No action needed.

Medium

Minor velocity dip OR scope growth <15% OR 1–2 blockers. PM monitors.

High

Velocity >15% decline OR scope >20% OR 3+ blockers. PM intervention; leader notified.

Critical

Customer commitment at risk OR company goal at risk. Immediate escalation.

High risk — Epic 'Enterprise SSO': Velocity declined 38% over 5 sprints. 2 blockers unresolved for 3+ sprints. Projected completion: June 15 (±2 weeks) — original target: May 1. 8 enterprise accounts ($2.8M ARR) expecting Q2 delivery.
Agent 3

Opportunity Spotter

Mission: Identify emerging customer needs and market opportunities the roadmap doesn't address.

Triggers

  • New theme: >10 mentions in 30 days
  • Rapid growth: >50% increase MoM
  • Competitor move creates demand
  • Underserved segment identified
  • Churn reveals unaddressed pain

Outputs

  • Opportunity cards (on threshold)
  • Gap analysis (weekly)
  • Demand trend reports (weekly)
  • Competitive response recommendations
  • ARR impact estimates

Trust Model

Progressive Trust

Earn user trust over time — from passive observation to delegated action.

1

Observer

AI: Monitors, surfaces alerts, provides data
User: Makes all decisions

"Here's what we see in customer feedback about Feature X"

2

Advisor

AI: Recommends with evidence
User: Approves or rejects

"We recommend re-prioritizing Initiative Y. Here's why."

3

Collaborator

AI: Drafts updates, generates reports
User: Reviews and approves

AI drafts weekly status; PM reviews and sends

4

Delegate

AI: Executes within boundaries
User: Sets boundaries

AI auto-updates dates from Jira; PM sets the policy

Launches at Level 1 + 2. Levels 3 & 4 unlock progressively based on accuracy, user opt-in, and org maturity.
