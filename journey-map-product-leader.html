Goal

Align a portfolio of products with company strategy, ensure teams are building the right things, and make fast portfolio-level decisions grounded in evidence rather than opinion.

AADHYA manages a portfolio of 3–8 product areas, each owned by a Product Manager. She reports to the CPO or CEO and is accountable for ensuring the product organization delivers on company strategy. Her daily work involves balancing strategic direction-setting with tactical unblocking, stakeholder alignment, and cross-team coordination.

Key Characteristics

Portfolio-level visibility Strategic theme focus Executive & PM interactions Strategy execution metrics Time is scarcest resource

Journey Phases

Click each phase to explore current-state pains and the dynamic roadmap future vision

1
Strategy Definition
Translating company vision into product strategy and OKRs
Frustrated
Current State
Activities
Translates company vision into product strategy; defines OKRs; creates strategic planning documents
Tools Used
Google Slides, spreadsheets, Aha!, Notion — disconnected from execution tools
Key Pain
Strategy and goals live in static documents with no live connection to what teams are actually building. Strategic intent gets lost in translation — teams interpret goals differently without a shared, connected system.
Workarounds
Manual check-ins with each PM to verify alignment; separate “strategy on a page” decks maintained alongside execution tools
Future Vision
Experience
Goals and OKRs defined directly in Miro, linked to portfolios and initiatives at every level
AI Role

Validates alignment in real-time, flagging when proposed work doesn't connect to active strategic objectives. Proactively alerts: “Initiative X has no connection to any active Q2 goal — consider re-evaluating or creating a new goal.”

Key Benefit
Strategy becomes a living, connected artifact — not a static document. Changes propagate automatically and alignment is continuously verified.
100% of initiatives linked to goals
2
Portfolio Oversight
Reviewing all product areas and allocating resources across initiatives
Overwhelmed
Current State
Activities
Reviews status of all product areas; identifies cross-team overlaps and gaps; allocates resources across initiatives
Tools Used
Individual Miro boards per PM, Jira dashboards, manual aggregation in spreadsheets
Key Pain
No shared visibility across products and teams. Each PM uses tools independently. Leaders must manually aggregate views across disparate boards, tools, and spreadsheets to form a portfolio picture.
Workarounds
Asks each PM to submit a weekly status update; manually creates a portfolio spreadsheet; uses Slack to chase updates
Future Vision
Experience
Unified portfolio view in Miro Spaces showing all product roadmaps with health signals, goal alignment scores, and delivery status. One-click drill-down from portfolio to individual roadmap to epic.
AI Role

Aggregates health signals across teams and surfaces anomalies: “Team Alpha's roadmap coverage of Goal 3 dropped from 40% to 15% after last week's re-prioritization.”

Key Benefit
Single pane of glass for the entire product portfolio — always current, always complete
Saves 3–5 hrs/week; 0 manual aggregation
“Each product manager currently uses Miro independently… we lack shared visibility across products and teams, which is why the Miro portfolio functionality looks promising.”
3
Decision Making
Prioritization calls, resource conflicts, and initiative scoping
Conflicted
Current State
Activities
Makes prioritization calls across product areas; resolves resource conflicts; approves or redirects initiative scope
Tools Used
Meetings, Slack debates, spreadsheet-based prioritization frameworks
Key Pain
Leadership over-involvement in tactical decisions; last-minute scope changes driven by opinions (HiPPO dynamics) rather than evidence. This undermines roadmap integrity and erodes team trust.
Workarounds
Holds extra 1:1s with PMs; requests ad-hoc analysis; defaults to the loudest voice in the room when data isn't available
Future Vision
Experience
Evidence-based decision support surfaced proactively. Leaders see customer evidence, execution reality, and strategic alignment for every initiative they're evaluating.
AI Role

Proactively surfaces decision-ready intelligence: “Customer churn signal in Segment X aligns with Initiative Y — recommend re-prioritizing. Here's the evidence: 8 accounts, $1.2M ARR, 23 feedback items in last 60 days.”

Key Benefit
Decisions are faster, more defensible, and grounded in evidence — reducing HiPPO dynamics and increasing team trust
50%+ reduction in decision cycle time
4
Stakeholder Alignment
Customizing roadmap views for board, executives, sales, and engineering
Exhausted
Current State
Activities
Prepares roadmap views for board meetings, executive reviews, sales kick-offs, engineering planning; responds to ad-hoc requests
Tools Used
PowerPoint/Google Slides, manually curated Miro boards, email updates
Key Pain
Teams spend hours manually customizing roadmap views for different stakeholders. Each audience needs a different level of detail and framing. Risk of version drift between different presentation layers.
Workarounds
Maintains 3–4 separate roadmap versions; dedicates a PM's time to “roadmap communications”; manually reconciles when versions drift
Future Vision
Experience
Auto-generated views for every audience — executive summary, team detail, customer-facing — all derived from one underlying data source. Each view updates automatically.
AI Role

Generates audience-appropriate summaries: “For tomorrow's board meeting, here's a 3-slide summary of Q1 progress, top risks, and key decisions needed.”

Key Benefit
Eliminates manual view creation; ensures all stakeholders see consistent, current information; recovers hours of leader time
Saves 5–10 hrs/month; 0 version drift
5
Progress Tracking
Tracking execution across teams and reporting status to leadership
Anxious
Current State
Activities
Tracks execution progress across teams; identifies delays and blockers; reports status to executive leadership
Tools Used
Jira dashboards (per team), manual status reports from PMs, spreadsheet trackers
Key Pain
Cannot understand if execution is on track. Relies on manual status reports from individual PMs. Information is always stale by the time it reaches leadership — surprises emerge too late to course-correct.
Workarounds
Requires PMs to send weekly written updates; joins team standups to get direct information; creates escalation protocols for late-breaking issues
Future Vision
Experience
Real-time delivery signals from Jira/Azure DevOps feed directly into the portfolio dashboard. Status is always current, always accurate.
AI Role

Flags risks and delays proactively: “Team Alpha's velocity dropped 25% over the last 3 sprints — Q2 deliverable for Initiative X is at risk. Recommended action: scope reduction or timeline adjustment.”

Key Benefit
Leaders see execution reality in real-time; risks are surfaced before they become crises; no more manual status aggregation
Eliminates 2–3 hrs/week of status gathering
6
Strategy Pivots
Responding to market changes and cascading strategy adjustments across teams
Stressed
Current State
Activities
Responds to market changes, executive mandates, or customer crises by adjusting product strategy; cascades changes across teams
Tools Used
Ad-hoc meetings, updated slide decks, Slack communications, manual Jira re-planning
Key Pain
Shifting product strategy requires cascading changes across multiple tools and teams. Impact analysis is manual and incomplete. Leaders can't see downstream effects of strategic pivots.
Workarounds
Holds war-room sessions to manually trace impact; asks each PM to assess impact independently; accepts that some downstream effects will be missed
Future Vision
Experience
Before committing to a pivot, leaders can simulate the impact across the portfolio. The system shows cascading effects on timelines, dependencies, customer commitments, and team capacity.
AI Role

Simulates strategy changes: “Shifting 30% of resources from Area A to Area B would: delay Initiatives X and Y by 6 weeks, affect 12 customer commitments ($3.2M ARR), but accelerate Goal 3 coverage by 40%.” Auto-proposes roadmap adjustments with trade-off analysis.

Key Benefit
Pivots become data-driven decisions with full visibility into consequences; reduces risk of unintended downstream effects
Pivot time: weeks → days; full blast radius visibility
